Executive Summary
Company Overview & Strategic Positioning

Power Test Global Technologies (hereinafter "PTGT" or "the Company") is a multinational enterprise IT integrator and digital transformation consultancy incorporated under the laws of the Grand Duchy of Luxembourg, with primary operational headquarters in Amsterdam, Netherlands, and principal delivery centers in Casablanca, Morocco and Dubai, United Arab Emirates. Founded in 2011, PTGT has grown from a regional IT services provider into a globally recognized partner for complex, mission-critical technology programs spanning public-sector modernization, financial systems transformation, healthcare digitization, and industrial IoT deployments.

As of fiscal year 2024, PTGT employs 2,847 full-time equivalents (FTEs) across 14 countries, maintains active contracts in 38 client organizations, and reports consolidated annual revenues of EUR 312.4 million with an EBITDA margin of 19.3%. The Company holds strategic vendor partnerships with Amazon Web Services, Microsoft Azure, Salesforce, SAP, and Oracle, and is certified under ISO 27001:2022, ISO 9001:2015, CMMI Level 4, and SOC 2 Type II frameworks.

Strategic Positioning

PTGT occupies a differentiated position in the global IT services market, operating at the intersection of deep technical engineering capability and sector-specific regulatory knowledge. Unlike generalist IT outsourcing firms, PTGT has deliberately concentrated its competencies in regulated-industry IT integration, enabling it to participate in markets — such as central bank infrastructure modernization, cross-border healthcare data exchange, and government cloud migration — that require both technical credibility and regulatory alignment.

The Company's competitive differentiation rests on three pillars: (1) a compliant-by-design engineering methodology that embeds legal and regulatory requirements into system architecture from day one; (2) a multilingual governance capability enabling direct engagement with regulatory authorities across European, Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC), and North African jurisdictions; and (3) a hybrid delivery model that combines onshore advisory expertise with cost-optimized nearshore and offshore engineering centers.

Market Role

PTGT serves as a Tier-1 systems integrator in the European and MENA (Middle East & North Africa) markets, regularly competing and partnering with global firms including Capgemini, CGI, Atos, and Deloitte Technology. The Company's revenue mix is approximately 55% from long-term managed services contracts, 30% from fixed-price implementation engagements, and 15% from time-and-materials consulting mandates. Public sector contracts account for 42% of total revenues, reflecting PTGT's strong positioning in government IT modernization programs.

Core Differentiators

  • Regulatory Embedded Engineering: PTGT's proprietary Compliance-First Development (CFD) methodology ensures that GDPR, NIS2, DORA, and sector-specific regulatory requirements are architected into solutions at the design stage, not added as post-implementation controls.
  • Multilingual Delivery: The Company delivers projects in English, French, Arabic, Dutch, and German, removing language barriers in cross-border engagements across the EU, GCC, and Maghreb regions.
  • Sovereign Cloud Expertise: PTGT holds rare technical certification and contractual frameworks enabling it to architect solutions on sovereign cloud infrastructure — a critical capability for EU GAIA-X participants and GCC national data residency requirements.
  • Ethics-by-Design AI Practice: The Company's Artificial Intelligence & Data Engineering practice operates under a published Ethical AI Charter, providing clients in regulated sectors with documented bias auditing, explainability, and model governance frameworks.
  • Capital Adequacy for Large Contracts: With a EUR 45 million revolving credit facility and a EUR 120 million balance sheet, PTGT meets the financial pre-qualification thresholds of the most demanding public procurement frameworks, including the EU Institutions' Financial Regulation (Article 196) and GCC government tender requirements.

1.2 Corporate Governance Model

PTGT adheres to a two-tier governance model consisting of a Supervisory Board (Conseil de Surveillance) and an Executive Management Board (Conseil d'Administration Exécutif), consistent with Luxembourg corporate law (Loi du 10 août 1915 sur les sociétés commerciales, as amended). This structure ensures clear separation between oversight and executive management functions, aligned with the OECD Principles of Corporate Governance (2023 Revision).

1.2.1 Supervisory Board

The Supervisory Board comprises five independent non-executive directors elected by shareholders at the Annual General Meeting (AGM). The Supervisory Board is responsible for approving the annual budget, material acquisitions (above EUR 10 million), changes to strategic direction, and the appointment of Executive Board members. Board meetings are held quarterly, with extraordinary meetings convened as required.

1.2.2 Executive Management Board

The Executive Management Board is responsible for the day-to-day management of the Company and consists of seven executive positions. The Executive Board reports to the Supervisory Board and prepares quarterly management accounts and a detailed annual report for shareholder review.

2.7 Export Controls & Government Contracting Limitations

2.7.1 Export Control Regime

PTGT products, services, and technology deliverables are subject to the following export control regimes:

  • EU Dual-Use Regulation (EU 2021/821): PTGT's encryption-capable software and cryptographic modules are classified under Category 5 (Telecommunications and Information Security) of the EU dual-use list. The Company holds a General Export Authorisation (GEA EU001) for exports to 8 approved countries. Exports to sanctioned jurisdictions (currently Russia, Belarus, Iran, North Korea, Myanmar) are strictly prohibited.
  • US EAR (Export Administration Regulations): Although PTGT is not a US entity, certain technology components licensed from US vendors (e.g., AWS GovCloud services, Oracle Database, certain cryptographic libraries) carry US-origin technology restrictions. PTGT's Legal & Compliance team conducts a Foreign Person screening for all project teams involving non-Western nationals accessing US-origin technology.
  • ITAR (International Traffic in Arms Regulations): PTGT is not registered with the US State Department's Directorate of Defense Trade Controls (DDTC) and is therefore categorically prohibited from handling ITAR-controlled technical data or defense articles. Any project scope that would require access to ITAR-controlled information must be declined.

2.7.2 Sanctions Screening

PTGT screens all new clients, counterparties, and key project stakeholders against the following sanctions lists before contract execution and on an ongoing monthly basis:

  • EU Consolidated Sanctions List (updated daily)
  • UN Security Council Consolidated List
  • US OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List
  • UK HM Treasury Consolidated List
  • UAE Executive Office AML/CFT Sanctions List

Screening is performed through the PTGT-contracted WorldCheck (Refinitiv) platform, with audit trails maintained for a minimum of seven years.

3.3 Legal Review Workflow Before Accepting a Project

Every project opportunity above EUR 250,000 (or equivalent) must pass through the following mandatory Legal Review Workflow (LRW) before any binding commitment is made. The LRW is managed through PTGT's CRM/contract management system (Salesforce CPQ) with mandatory gate approvals:

Stage 1 — Initial Opportunity Assessment (Business Development)

The CRO team completes a standard Opportunity Assessment Form (OAF-01) covering: client identity, proposed project scope, contracting jurisdiction, estimated contract value, sector classification, data types involved, and any known regulatory complexity. The OAF-01 is submitted to the Compliance team within 48 hours of receiving an invitation to tender or direct approach.

Stage 2 — KYC/Sanctions Screening (Compliance Team)

The AML/KYC Analyst conducts a WorldCheck screening of the client entity, its ultimate beneficial owners (to 25% ownership threshold), and its key decision-makers. Results are documented in the Client Due Diligence Record (CDDR). Any adverse finding triggers Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) and requires CLCO sign-off before proceeding.

Stage 3 — Eligibility Analysis (Contract Counsel + Compliance Manager)

Using the Eligibility Decision Framework (Section 2.8), contract counsel confirms whether the project falls within PTGT's authorized service scope, identifies any applicable restrictions, and determines whether special authorizations are required. An Eligibility Report (ER-Form) is produced within 5 business days.

Stage 4 — Financial Viability Check (CFO Office)

For contracts above EUR 5 million, the CFO office reviews the capital adequacy position (Section 2.8.4), insurance coverage adequacy, and revenue recognition implications under IFRS 15. A formal Go/No-Go Financial Memorandum is issued.

Stage 5 — CLCO Sign-Off

The CLCO reviews all outputs from Stages 1–4 and issues a final Legal Clearance Certificate (LCC) authorizing the sales team to submit a binding proposal. If any unresolved compliance issue exists, the LCC is withheld pending resolution. The CLCO may attach conditions or restrictions to the LCC that must be reflected in the final contract.

Stage 6 — Executive Approval (for Large Contracts)

Contracts above EUR 25 million additionally require CEO + CFO + CLCO co-signatures. Contracts above EUR 50 million require Supervisory Board resolution. Emergency approvals may be granted by the CEO alone for time-sensitive bids, subject to post-approval ratification within 5 business days.
